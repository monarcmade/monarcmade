# Monarc University - Full Cohort Blueprint

## Positioning

Monarc University teaches everyday builders, founders, professionals, and career switchers how to understand AI, use it with confidence, and build real AI-powered systems.

The promise:

> Go from AI beginner to practical builder with a shipped AI project, a repeatable workflow system, and the confidence to use AI for business, career growth, or client work.

This is not a random prompt course. It is a guided builder cohort.

## Core Audience

### Primary Student

- Wants to learn AI but feels behind
- Has heard about ChatGPT, agents, automations, and AI tools but does not know where to start
- Wants practical skills that can help with work, business, content, freelancing, or a career pivot
- May not consider themselves deeply technical yet
- Needs structure, accountability, examples, and plain-language teaching

### Secondary Student

- Founder or business owner who wants to use AI inside operations
- Creative or service provider who wants to make content, automate admin work, or create offers
- Early developer who wants to move into AI apps, agents, and automations
- Professional who wants to become the AI person on their team

## Program Structure

Monarc University can be sold in two formats.

### 12-Week Fast Track

Best for students who want speed, accountability, and a clear transformation.

- Length: 12 weeks
- Weekly time: 6-10 hours
- Live sessions: 2 per week
- Structure: lesson, demo, lab, assignment, review
- Output: shipped capstone project

### 24-Week Standard Track

Best for beginners, busy professionals, and students who need more time.

- Length: 24 weeks
- Weekly time: 3-5 hours
- Live sessions: 1 per week
- Structure: concept, demo, lab, project review
- Output: shipped capstone project

## Curriculum Spine

Both tracks use the same six-phase spine. The fast track spends two weeks per phase. The standard track spends four weeks per phase.

| Phase | Fast Track | Standard Track | Outcome |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| 1. AI Foundations | Weeks 1-2 | Weeks 1-4 | Understand how AI works and how to use it clearly |
| 2. Prompt Systems | Weeks 3-4 | Weeks 5-8 | Build repeatable AI workflows, not one-off prompts |
| 3. AI APIs & Apps | Weeks 5-6 | Weeks 9-12 | Connect AI to real apps and tools |
| 4. RAG & Knowledge Systems | Weeks 7-8 | Weeks 13-16 | Build assistants that answer from documents/data |
| 5. Agents & Automations | Weeks 9-10 | Weeks 17-20 | Build assistants that use tools and trigger workflows |
| 6. Production & Capstone | Weeks 11-12 | Weeks 21-24 | Ship, demo, and package a real AI project |

## Module Breakdown

### Phase 1: AI Foundations

Goal: Make AI feel understandable and usable.

Lessons:

- What AI actually is
- How LLMs work in plain English
- Tokens, context windows, models, and hallucinations
- What AI is good at and bad at
- How to choose the right AI tool
- The difference between consumer AI, business AI, and builder AI

Student deliverables:

- Personal AI use-case map
- AI tool stack audit
- First workflow prompt
- Before/after productivity example

Marketing videos:

- How I'd Learn AI From Zero in 2026
- AI Explained for People Who Don't Code
- The AI Tools I Would Learn First

### Phase 2: Prompt Systems

Goal: Move students from random prompting to repeatable systems.

Lessons:

- Prompt structure
- Role, context, task, format, examples
- Prompt iteration
- Structured outputs
- Content prompts vs. business prompts
- Building prompt libraries
- Turning one prompt into a workflow

Student deliverables:

- Personal prompt library
- Business workflow prompt
- Content creation prompt system
- AI assistant instruction file

Marketing videos:

- Stop Writing Prompts. Build AI Workflows Instead.
- The Prompt Formula I Use for Real Client Work
- How to Make AI Sound Like You

### Phase 3: AI APIs & Apps

Goal: Teach students how AI moves from chat window to real product.

Lessons:

- What an API is
- How OpenAI and Claude APIs work
- Inputs, outputs, temperature, system prompts
- Streaming responses
- Cost and token management
- Simple AI app architecture
- Connecting AI to forms, dashboards, and tools

Student deliverables:

- First AI-powered form
- Simple AI assistant prototype
- API cost calculator
- Mini app demo

Marketing videos:

- How to Build Your First AI App
- ChatGPT Is Not the Product. The Workflow Is.
- How AI Apps Actually Work Behind the Scenes

### Phase 4: RAG & Knowledge Systems

Goal: Help students build AI that answers from real knowledge, not guesses.

Lessons:

- What RAG is
- Embeddings in plain English
- Chunking documents
- Vector databases
- Retrieval and ranking
- Uploading PDFs, SOPs, docs, and website content
- Building a knowledge assistant

Student deliverables:

- Document-based chatbot
- Business knowledge base map
- RAG prototype
- Evaluation checklist for answer quality

Marketing videos:

- How to Make AI Answer From Your Own Documents
- Build an AI Assistant for Your Business Files
- Why ChatGPT Gets Things Wrong and How RAG Fixes It

### Phase 5: Agents & Automations

Goal: Teach students how AI can take action through tools and workflows.

Lessons:

- What AI agents are
- Tool calling
- Agent loops
- Human-in-the-loop review
- Slack, email, calendar, forms, CRM, and spreadsheet automations
- Designing safe automation workflows
- When not to use agents

Student deliverables:

- AI workflow automation
- Agent design document
- Human review checklist
- Tool-connected assistant prototype

Marketing videos:

- AI Agents Explained for Normal People
- I Built an AI Assistant That Does My Repetitive Work
- The Difference Between AI Automation and AI Agents

### Phase 6: Production & Capstone

Goal: Help students ship something real.

Lessons:

- Project scoping
- User stories
- Data and privacy considerations
- Evals and regression checks
- Logging and observability
- Deployment basics
- Demo and portfolio packaging
- Selling or using the project

Student deliverables:

- Final capstone project
- Capstone project document
- Demo video
- Portfolio/client case study
- Launch plan

Marketing videos:

- I Built an AI Tool From Scratch. Here's the Full Process.
- What Your First AI Project Should Be
- How to Turn an AI Project Into a Paid Offer

## Weekly Delivery Rhythm

Use the software development cohort as the delivery reference.

Each week should include:

- Lesson deck
- Live teaching session
- Live demo or walkthrough
- Lab exercise
- Assignment
- Office hours or review
- Student checkpoint
- Optional stretch challenge

The standard rhythm:

1. Teach the concept
2. Show the tool/workflow
3. Build a small example
4. Give students a lab
5. Review the result
6. Connect it to the capstone

## Capstone Options

Students should pick one capstone by the middle of the program.

Strong capstone tracks:

- AI content engine
- Client intake assistant
- Lead qualification assistant
- Proposal generator
- Business document chatbot
- SOP knowledge assistant
- Customer support response assistant
- Recruiting/resume assistant
- Personal productivity operating system
- AI-powered service dashboard

Capstone document sections:

- Problem statement
- Target user
- Current process
- Desired process
- AI role
- Tools used
- Data sources
- Risks and guardrails
- Build plan
- Demo script
- Outcome and next steps
